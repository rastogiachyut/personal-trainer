## Cold Exposure

- **Cold water immersion (8-15°C for 10-15 min) increases norepinephrine by 250-530% and dopamine by 250%,** with effects lasting 2-4 hours post-exposure. — [Huberman Lab / PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=cold+water+immersion+norepinephrine)
- **Cold showers (2-3 min at 10-15°C) provide ~60% of the dopamine response of full cold plunges,** making them a practical daily alternative. — [Huberman Lab](https://www.hubermanlab.com)
- **Cold exposure activates brown adipose tissue (BAT),** which increases resting metabolic rate by 5-15% for several hours. BAT is more active in lean individuals and can be increased through regular cold exposure. — [PubMed 2024](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=brown+adipose+tissue+cold+exposure+metabolic+rate)
- **Chronic cold exposure (3+ sessions/week) may blunt hypertrophy adaptations when done within 1 hour post-resistance training.** Cold immersion shifts the inflammatory response away from muscle growth pathways. — [PubMed Meta-Analysis](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=cold+water+immersion+hypertrophy+blunting)
- **Cold exposure reduces DOMS by ~20-40% when used within 1-4 hours after intense exercise,** but this acute recovery benefit conflicts with long-term hypertrophy goals. — [PubMed Cochrane Review](https://www.cochranelibrary.com/)
- **Optimal protocol for general health (not post-workout): 1-3 min at 50-60°F (10-15°C), 2-4 times per week, ideally in the morning.** Avoid within 4 hours of bedtime as it can disrupt sleep. — [Huberman Lab](https://www.hubermanlab.com)
- **Cold plunging before exercise (pre-cooling) improves endurance performance in hot environments by 4-15%** by reducing core temperature and delaying heat accumulation. — [PubMed 2024](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=pre-cooling+exercise+performance)
- **Cold exposure increases fat oxidation acutely** by activating lipolysis in white fat cells via the sympathetic nervous system. Regular cold exposure may raise basal metabolic rate long-term. — [PubMed 2023](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=cold+exposure+lipolysis+fat+oxidation)
- **Whole-body cryotherapy (-110°C for 2-3 min) shows similar recovery benefits to cold water immersion but at much higher cost.** Evidence does not support superiority over simple cold baths. — [PubMed Review](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=whole+body+cryotherapy+recovery)
- **Cold exposure protocol for metabolic benefits: progress from cold showers (1-2 min) → ice baths (5-10 min at 10-15°C) → full plunges.** Never push past shivering + gasping; hyperventilation is normal in first 30 seconds. — [Huberman Lab](https://www.hubermanlab.com)

## Heat Exposure (Sauna)

- **Finnish sauna use 4-7 times per week at 80°C+ for 20+ minutes reduces all-cause mortality by 40% compared to 1x/week users** (Laukkanen cohort study, 2,300 middle-aged men, 20-year follow-up). — [PubMed / FoundMyFitness](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27433114/)
- **Regular sauna use (4-7x/week) reduces cardiovascular disease mortality by ~50% and is associated with lower blood pressure, improved endothelial function, and reduced arterial stiffness.** — [PubMed 2018](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=sauna+cardiovascular+mortality)
- **Sauna use increases growth hormone by 2-5x acutely,** with two 20-minute sessions at 80°C separated by a 30-min cooling period producing the largest GH response (~5x baseline). — [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=sauna+growth+hormone)
- **Heat exposure increases heat shock proteins (HSPs)** which protect against protein misfolding, reduce inflammation, and may slow cellular aging. HSP70 increases by ~50% after 30 min at 80°C. — [PubMed 2024](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=heat+shock+proteins+sauna+aging)
- **Post-exercise sauna bathing improves endurance performance adaptations** by increasing plasma volume and improving thermoregulation. Systematic review found positive effects on VO2 max and time to exhaustion. — [PubMed 2025 - Ahokas et al.](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41032138/)
- **Passive heating (hot water immersion or sauna) shows mixed cardiometabolic results in RCTs:** 2025 meta-analysis found no significant pooled effects for flow-mediated dilation, blood pressure, or cholesterol across 20 RCTs. Observational data is stronger than RCT evidence. — [PubMed 2025 - Hamaya et al.](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41049507/)
- **Sauna protocol: 175-210°F (80-99°C) for 15-30 minutes, 3-4 times per week minimum for health benefits.** Add a 1-2 min cold plunge between sauna sessions for contrast therapy. — [FoundMyFitness / Rhonda Patrick](https://foundmyfitness.com)
- **Infrared saunas (120-140°F / 50-60°C) produce similar cardiovascular benefits at lower temperatures,** making them accessible for those who can't tolerate Finnish sauna heat. Longer sessions needed (30-45 min). — [PubMed Review](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=infrared+sauna+health+benefits)
- **Sauna use mimics cardiovascular exercise:** heart rate increases to 120-150 bpm, cardiac output increases 60-70%, and sweat rate reaches 0.5-1.0 kg in a typical session. — [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=sauna+cardiovascular+response)

## Sleep Optimization

- **Optimal sleep for physical recovery: 7-9 hours per night.** Sleep restriction (<6h) reduces testosterone 10-15%, increases cortisol, reduces insulin sensitivity, and increases hunger hormones (ghrelin up, leptin down). — [PubMed 2025 Meta-Analysis](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=sleep+restriction+testosterone+cortisol)
- **Each hour of sleep below 7 hours/night is associated with 0.35 kg/m² increase in BMI** and 33% increased obesity risk. — [CDC](https://www.cdc.gov/obesity/risk-factors/risk-factors.html)
- **Sleep deprivation (<6 hours) reduces strength performance by 5-10% and delays recovery by 30-50%.** — [PubMed 2025](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=sleep+deprivation+strength+performance)
- **Core body temperature must drop ~1°C (1.8°F) to initiate and maintain deep sleep.** Optimal bedroom temperature: 60-67°F (15.5-19.5°C). Warmer temperatures reduce REM and deep sleep. — [Huberman Lab / Sleep Research](https://www.hubermanlab.com)
- **Viewing bright light (sunlight or 10,000 lux SAD lamp) within 30-60 minutes of waking triggers cortisol and dopamine release,** entraining the circadian rhythm and improving evening sleep quality. — [Huberman Lab](https://www.hubermanlab.com)
- **Avoid bright light (especially overhead and blue light) between 10 PM and 4 AM** to prevent suppression of melatonin. Use dim, low-placed lights (red/amber) after sunset. — [Huberman Lab](https://www.hubermanlab.com)
- **Caffeine has a quarter-life of ~12 hours:** caffeine consumed at noon still has 25% active in the bloodstream at midnight, occupying adenosine receptors and degrading sleep quality even if you fall asleep. Optimal cutoff: 8-10 hours before bedtime. — [Huberman Lab / Examine.com](https://examine.com/supplements/caffeine/)
- **Magnesium glycinate (200-400mg) before bed improves sleep onset and quality** in deficient individuals (~50% of adults are magnesium-deficient). Glycinate form is best for sleep (calming, well-absorbed). — [Examine.com](https://examine.com/supplements/magnesium/)
- **Glycine (3g before bed) lowers core body temperature and improves sleep quality,** reducing time to fall asleep and improving subjective sleep quality scores. — [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=glycine+sleep+quality)
- **Apigenin (50mg), found in chamomile, acts as a mild sedative via GABA receptors.** Combining with magnesium glycinate enhances sleep-inducing effects without grogginess. — [Examine.com](https://examine.com/supplements/apigenin/)
- **Inositol (900mg-2g before bed) improves sleep quality,** particularly in individuals with anxiety or OCD tendencies. Also helps with insulin sensitivity. — [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=inositol+sleep)
- **Mouth taping (using medical-grade porous tape) encourages nasal breathing during sleep,** which increases nitric oxide production, improves oxygenation, and reduces snoring/sleep apnea severity. — [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=mouth+taping+sleep+apnea)
- **Alcohol consumed within 3 hours of bedtime suppresses REM sleep by 20-40%** and causes sleep fragmentation in the second half of the night as it metabolizes. — [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=alcohol+REM+sleep+suppression)
- **NSDR (Non-Sleep Deep Rest) / Yoga Nidra for 10-20 minutes during the day** reduces cortisol, replenishes dopamine, and can substitute for ~30 min of nighttime sleep. — [Huberman Lab](https://www.hubermanlab.com/nsdr)

## Light Therapy

- **Morning sunlight exposure (5-10 min outdoors, no sunglasses) within 30-60 min of waking** is the single most powerful circadian entrainment signal. Triggers cortisol pulse, increases daytime alertness, and improves evening sleep. — [Huberman Lab](https://www.hubermanlab.com)
- **Red light therapy (630-670nm red + 810-850nm near-infrared) at 4-15 J/cm² improves skin health, reduces inflammation, and enhances mitochondrial function.** Typical protocol: 10-20 min, 2-5x/week, 6-12 inches from device. — [PubMed 2024 Meta-Analysis](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=red+light+therapy+photobiomodulation+meta-analysis)
- **Photobiomodulation (PBM) research is rapidly growing:** PubMed shows 24 meta-analyses published 2023-2025, with strongest evidence for skin healing, pain reduction, and ophthalmic applications. — [PubMed Search Results](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=red+light+therapy+photobiomodulation+meta-analysis&filter=dates.2023%2F1%2F1-2025%2F12%2F31)
- **Blue light blocking glasses (amber/red lenses) 2-3 hours before bed improve sleep onset latency and sleep quality** by allowing natural melatonin production. Clear "computer glasses" that block only partial blue light are less effective. — [PubMed Meta-Analysis](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=blue+light+blocking+glasses+sleep)
- **Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD) responds to 10,000 lux light therapy for 20-30 minutes within 1 hour of waking.** Effective for ~60-80% of SAD sufferers within 1-2 weeks. — [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=seasonal+affective+disorder+light+therapy)
- **Sunlight exposure to skin (UVB) produces vitamin D3:** 10-30 min of midday sun on arms/legs/face, 3-5x/week, is sufficient for most people. Darker skin requires 3-5x longer exposure. — [WHO / PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=vitamin+D+sunlight+synthesis)

## Breathwork

- **The "physiological sigh" (double inhale through nose, long exhale through mouth) is the fastest evidence-based method to reduce acute stress.** 1-3 cycles lower heart rate and calm the nervous system within seconds. Discovered by Huberman/Krasnow lab at Stanford. — [Huberman Lab / Stanford](https://www.hubermanlab.com)
- **Box breathing (4-4-4-4: inhale 4s, hold 4s, exhale 4s, hold 4s) for 5 minutes reduces cortisol and improves focus.** Used by Navy SEALs for stress regulation. — [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=box+breathing+stress+reduction)
- **Wim Hof Method (breathing + cold exposure + meditation) voluntarily influences the autonomic nervous system and immune response.** Studies show increased epinephrine, reduced inflammatory markers (IL-6, IL-10) after controlled hyperventilation + breath holds. — [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=wim+hof+method+immune+response)
- **Nasal breathing during exercise increases nitric oxide production 6-fold,** improving oxygen utilization, vascular function, and reducing perceived exertion at moderate intensities. — [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=nasal+breathing+nitric+oxide+exercise)
- **Slow breathing (5-6 breaths per minute) maximally stimulates the vagus nerve** and produces the strongest parasympathetic (rest and digest) response. This is "resonance frequency breathing." — [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=resonance+frequency+breathing+vagal)
- **Breath holds after exhale (Buteyko-style) can improve asthma symptoms and reduce need for medication** by raising CO2 tolerance and reducing hyperventilation. Controversial but some evidence supports it. — [Cochrane / PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=buteyko+breathing+asthma)
- **Mouth breathing during sleep reduces oxygen saturation and is associated with higher rates of sleep apnea, snoring, and dental problems.** Nasal breathing should be the default. — [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=mouth+breathing+sleep+apnea)

## Supplements & Nootropics

- **Ashwagandha (300-600mg KSM-66 daily) reduces cortisol by 15-25% and may modestly increase testosterone (10-15%) in stressed or training men.** Best taken with food. Effects appear after 4-8 weeks of consistent use. — [Examine.com](https://examine.com/supplements/ashwagandha/)
- **Creatine monohydrate (3-5g daily) has emerging cognitive benefits:** improves memory, reasoning, and mental fatigue under sleep deprivation or stress. Brain creatine stores are lower than muscle and respond to supplementation. — [PubMed 2024](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=creatine+cognitive+performance)
- **Omega-3 fatty acids (2-4g combined EPA+DHA daily) reduce inflammation, support brain health, and may reduce muscle soreness.** The EPA:DHA ratio should be ≥2:1 for anti-inflammatory effects. — [Examine.com](https://examine.com/supplements/fish-oil/)
- **Vitamin D3 (1000-4000 IU daily) deficiency affects 40-60% of populations and is associated with reduced testosterone, impaired recovery, lower mood, and weaker immune function.** Test levels first; supplement to maintain 30-50 ng/mL. — [Examine.com](https://examine.com/supplements/vitamin-d/)
- **Magnesium glycinate or citrate (200-400mg elemental magnesium) improves sleep, recovery, and reduces muscle cramps.** ~50% of adults are deficient. Glycinate for sleep, citrate for absorption, threonate for cognitive benefits. — [Examine.com](https://examine.com/supplements/magnesium/)
- **NMN (nicotinamide mononucleotide) and NR (nicotinamide riboside) increase NAD+ levels in humans.** MIB-626 (a crystalline NMN formulation) increased circulating NAD+ and its metabolome in middle-aged and older adults. Long-term health benefits in humans still being researched. — [PubMed 2023 - Pencina et al.](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/35182418/)
- **L-theanine (200-400mg) combined with caffeine (100-200mg) produces calm, focused attention** without the jitteriness of caffeine alone. Ratio of 2:1 theanine:caffeine is optimal. — [Examine.com](https://examine.com/supplements/l-theanine/)
- **Rhodiola rosea (200-600mg standardized extract) reduces fatigue and improves stress resilience,** particularly beneficial during periods of high physical or mental stress. — [Examine.com](https://examine.com/supplements/rhodiola/)
- **Zinc (15-30mg daily) deficiency reduces testosterone and impairs immune function.** ~17% of global population is deficient. Best absorbed with food; avoid taking with calcium or iron (competition). — [Examine.com](https://examine.com/supplements/zinc/)
- **Collagen peptides (10-15g daily) improve joint, tendon, and skin health over 3-6 months,** but are NOT effective for muscle building (very low leucine). Best consumed with vitamin C (50mg+) which is required for collagen synthesis. — [Examine.com](https://examine.com/supplements/collagen/)
- **Lion's Mane mushroom (500-3000mg daily) may stimulate nerve growth factor (NGF) production** and support cognitive function and memory. Evidence is promising but mostly from animal studies; human trials are small. — [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=lions+mane+nerve+growth+factor)
- **Tart cherry extract (480mg or 8-12oz juice, 2x/day) reduces DOMS, oxidative stress, and inflammation** via anthocyanins. Also naturally contains melatonin, improving sleep. — [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=tart+cherry+muscle+soreness)
- **Beta-alanine (3.2-6.4g daily) buffers lactic acid and improves performance for 60-240 second activities.** Causes harmless tingling (paresthesia). Benefits are small (~1-3%) but consistent. — [Examine.com](https://examine.com/supplements/beta-alanine/)
- **Citrulline malate (6-8g pre-workout) increases nitric oxide production, reduces fatigue, and increases training volume by 5-10%.** Superior to arginine (which is degraded by the liver). — [Examine.com](https://examine.com/supplements/citrulline/)
- **Metformin (off-label anti-aging): animal studies show lifespan extension, but human evidence is mixed.** May blunt exercise adaptations (mitochondrial benefits). Peter Attia discontinued metformin due to this concern. — [Peter Attia / PubMed](https://peterattiamd.com)
- **Rapamycin (off-label): weekly dosing (3-6mg) extends lifespan in mice by 15-25%.** Human trials ongoing. Side effects include immunosuppression, wound healing issues. Experimental — not recommended without medical supervision. — [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=rapamycin+anti-aging+human)

## Hormone Optimization

- **Every 1 kg of body fat lost increases testosterone by ~1-2 nmol/L in overweight men.** Losing 20+ kg can restore low testosterone to normal range without medication. — [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=weight+loss+testosterone+obese+men)
- **Sleep is the #1 natural testosterone booster:** 5 hours/night for 1 week reduces testosterone by 10-15%. Optimal: 7.5-9 hours in a cool, dark room. — [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=sleep+restriction+testosterone+reduction)
- **Heavy compound lifts (squats, deadlifts, bench press) acutely increase testosterone by 15-40% for 1-2 hours post-exercise.** This acute spike doesn't drive hypertrophy directly but indicates healthy hormonal function. — [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=resistance+training+acute+testosterone)
- **Vitamin D + Zinc + Magnesium combination supports natural testosterone production** when deficiencies are corrected. All three are commonly deficient in overweight individuals. — [Examine.com](https://examine.com)
- **Chronic stress elevates cortisol, which is antagonistic to testosterone.** Cortisol and testosterone share the same precursor (pregnenolone); high cortisol = lower testosterone. — [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=cortisol+testosterone+antagonistic)
- **Avoid endocrine disruptors:** BPA and phthalates (plastics), parabens (cosmetics), and triclosan (antibacterial products) are anti-androgenic. Use glass containers, avoid heating food in plastic. — [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=endocrine+disruptors+testosterone)

## Metabolic Health

- **Zone 2 cardio (60-70% max HR, conversational pace) for 180+ min/week improves mitochondrial density and metabolic flexibility.** This is the foundation of Peter Attia's exercise framework for longevity. — [Peter Attia](https://peterattiamd.com/exercise-longevity/)
- **VO2 max is the single strongest predictor of all-cause mortality:** moving from bottom 25% to top 25% of cardiorespiratory fitness reduces all-cause mortality risk by 50-70%. — [Peter Attia / PubMed](https://peterattiamd.com/vo2-max/)
- **Continuous Glucose Monitors (CGMs) reveal that "healthy" foods can cause dangerous glucose spikes in some individuals.** Personalized nutrition based on CGM data shows that glycemic responses vary 3-4x between individuals eating identical meals. — [PubMed / Stanford study](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=continuous+glucose+monitor+personalized+nutrition)
- **Insulin resistance precedes type 2 diabetes by 10-15 years.** Waist circumference >40" (men) is a strong predictor. Redcing body fat, particularly visceral fat, is the most effective intervention. — [WHO / PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=insulin+resistance+visceral+fat)
- **Time-restricted eating (8-10 hour eating window) improves insulin sensitivity, reduces blood pressure, and increases fat oxidation** independent of calorie restriction. — [PubMed Meta-Analysis](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=time-restricted+eating+insulin+sensitivity)
- **Metabolic flexibility (ability to efficiently burn both carbs and fat) is improved by:** Zone 2 cardio, intermittent fasting, low-carb periods, and avoiding constant snacking. — [Peter Attia](https://peterattiamd.com)
- **Postprandial walks (10-15 min after meals) reduce blood glucose spikes by 15-30%** by increasing GLUT4-mediated glucose uptake in muscles. — [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=postprandial+walking+glucose)

## Recovery Technology

- **Foam rolling (self-myofascial release) for 90-120 seconds per muscle group reduces DOMS and improves short-term flexibility** but evidence for long-term fascial changes is weak. Primary benefit is acute tissue preparation and soreness reduction. — [PubMed Review](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=foam+rolling+self-myofascial+release)
- **Massage guns (percussive therapy) show similar acute benefits to foam rolling** for reducing muscle soreness and improving ROM. No evidence they "break up scar tissue" or provide benefits beyond temporary increased blood flow. — [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=percussive+therapy+massage+gun)
- **Compression boots (Normatec, etc.) may reduce perceived soreness and improve recovery perception,** but objective performance recovery evidence is weak. Most beneficial for athletes with high training volumes. — [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=compression+therapy+recovery+athletes)
- **PEMF (Pulsed Electromagnetic Field) therapy shows moderate evidence for bone healing and pain reduction,** but evidence for performance recovery is limited. — [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=PEMF+recovery+athletes)
- **EMS/TENS devices can maintain muscle mass during injury immobilization** (preventing atrophy) but are not a replacement for voluntary contraction for hypertrophy. — [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=EMS+muscle+atrophy+rehabilitation)
- **Whoop/Oura/Apple Watch sleep tracking is ~80-85% accurate for sleep stage detection** compared to polysomnography. Most accurate for total sleep time and least accurate for REM/deep sleep distinction. — [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=wearable+sleep+tracker+accuracy)

## Longevity Protocols

- **Peter Attia's exercise framework for longevity (in priority order):** 1) VO2 max training (4x4 intervals), 2) Strength training (2-3x/week), 3) Zone 2 cardio (180+ min/week), 4) Stability/mobility work. Exercise is the #1 longevity intervention, far exceeding any supplement or drug. — [Peter Attia](https://peterattiamd.com/exercise-longevity/)
- **Grip strength is a powerful biomarker: each 5kg decrease is associated with 16% increase in all-cause mortality risk.** Easily measurable with a dynamometer. — [Peter Attia / PubMed](https://peterattiamd.com/grip-strength/)
- **Muscle mass is inversely associated with all-cause mortality:** each 10% increase in skeletal muscle mass index is associated with a 6% decrease in mortality risk. — [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=muscle+mass+all-cause+mortality)
- **Resistance training preserves bone density, muscle mass (sarcopenia prevention), and metabolic health into old age.** Weight-bearing exercise at >85% 1RM is required for osteogenesis (bone formation). — [Peter Attia / PubMed](https://peterattiamd.com/resistance-training-longevity/)
- **Huberman's "Fundamental Five" weekly protocol:** 1) 150-200 min Zone 2 cardio, 2) 75+ min VO2 max intervals, 3) 2-3x resistance training, 4) 150-200 jumps (bone density), 5) 10-15 min/day mobility. — [Huberman Lab](https://www.hubermanlab.com)
- **Caloric restriction (20-30% deficit) extends lifespan in multiple species** but is difficult to sustain. Alternate-day fasting and 5:2 protocols produce similar metabolic benefits without continuous restriction. — [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=caloric+restriction+lifespan+human)
- **Bryan Johnson's Blueprint protocol (100+ daily supplements, strict diet, tracking) is extreme but provides a data-rich template.** Most evidence-based elements: creatine, omega-3, vitamin D, magnesium, NAD+ precursors, metformin (debated), statins (if prescribed). — [blueprint.bryanjohnson.com](https://blueprint.bryanjohnson.com)
- **The biggest longevity levers (in order of evidence strength):** 1) Exercise (cardio + strength), 2) Sleep, 3) Diet quality, 4) Stress management, 5) Social connection, 6) Supplements, 7) Pharmacological interventions. — [Peter Attia](https://peterattiamd.com)
- **Sitting >8 hours/day is associated with 15-20% increased cardiovascular mortality,** but this is eliminated by 60-75 min of daily moderate exercise. — [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=sedentary+sitting+mortality+exercise)
- **150 jumps per week (box jumps, jump rope) significantly improves bone mineral density** in hips and spine. Critical for aging; bone density loss accelerates after 40. — [Huberman Lab / PubMed](https://www.hubermanlab.com)
