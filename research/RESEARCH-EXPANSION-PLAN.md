## 🎯 Expansion Targets

### Phase 1: New Research Domains (Not Yet Covered)

#### 1.1 — Advanced Training Techniques
**Topics to research:**
- [ ] **Blood Flow Restriction (BFR) Training** — occlusion training for hypertrophy at low loads (15-30% 1RM). Proven effective in clinical settings. Could be a game-changer for joint-friendly muscle growth at 130 kg.
- [ ] **Eccentric Training & Tempo Work** — slowing down the lowering phase (3-5 sec eccentrics) for greater muscle damage and hypertrophy. Especially useful for breaking plateaus.
- [ ] **Partial Reps & Lengthened Partial Training** — 2024-2025 research showing training muscles at long lengths produces superior hypertrophy vs full ROM in some exercises.
- [ ] **Myo-reps / Rest-Pause Training** — time-efficient intensity techniques for when sessions need to be shorter.
- [ ] **Cluster Sets** — intra-set rest (10-15s between reps) for maintained power output and total volume.
- [ ] **Pre-exhaustion & Post-exhaustion Supersets** — pairing compound + isolation for time efficiency.
- [ ] **Isometric Training** — pauses at sticking points, overcoming isometrics for strength plateaus.

**Target output:** 30-40 new facts → `research/facts-advanced-training.md/json`

- [ ] **Pre/intra/post-workout nutrition windows** — how much does timing actually matter? (Spoiler: less than supplement companies claim, but there are optimizations.)

- [ ] **Agonist-antagonist supersets** — pairing push/pull (bench + row) to save time without performance loss. 2024 meta-analysis support.
- [ ] **The "2.5 kg plate rule"** — micro-loading for upper body lifts where 5 kg jumps are too much. Fractional plates or 1.25 kg plates.
- [ ] **Mind-muscle connection** — focusing on the target muscle during reps increases EMG activation and hypertrophy (Schoenfeld 2018+ studies).
- [ ] **Tempo manipulation** — when to use slow eccentrics, when to use explosive concentrics, when to pause.

## 🛠️ Methodology

### Research Process Per Topic

```
1. Web search → find latest meta-analyses, systematic reviews, expert consensus (2023-2026)
2. Fetch 3-5 high-quality sources per topic
3. Extract verifiable facts with citations
4. Cross-reference across sources (require ≥2 sources for controversial claims)
5. Rate evidence quality:
   🟢 STRONG — meta-analysis, RCT, or broad expert consensus
   🟡 MODERATE — individual RCTs, mechanistic reasoning, limited studies
   🔴 WEAK — observational, anecdotal, single source, emerging
6. Save as structured markdown + JSON
7. Commit to repo
8. Update workout generation principles if applicable
```
