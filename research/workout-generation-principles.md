# 🧠 Workout Generation Principles

**Created:** July 7, 2026
**Purpose:** Document the evidence-based principles and research used to generate Achyut's workout sessions

---

## 1. Body Recomposition Strategy (Not Pure Cutting)

**Principle:** At 130 kg, beginner status, and high body fat, the goal is simultaneous fat loss + muscle gain — not pure weight loss.

**Research basis:**
- Beginners with high body fat are ideal candidates for recomposition (Renaissance Periodization)
- Resistance training during caloric restriction preserves ~2-3 kg of lean mass vs diet alone (Cochrane)
- Combined diet + exercise produces 2-3 kg more long-term weight loss than diet alone (Cochrane)

**Application:** Workouts prioritize muscle-building stimulus (not high-rep "toning" nonsense). Caloric deficit comes from nutrition, not from doing 20-rep sets.

**Source:** `research/facts-database.md` → Body Recomposition section; `research/weight-loss/comprehensive-guide.md`

## 2. Progressive Overload (The Core Engine)

**Principle:** Every session, attempt to do more than last time — more weight, more reps, or better form. This is the single most important driver of muscle growth.

**Research basis:**
- Progressive overload is the primary mechanism for hypertrophy and strength gains (Stronger By Science)
- Double progression model: add reps within range → add weight → reset reps (Renaissance Periodization)
- Even small increases (1-2.5 kg) sustained over months produce significant cumulative gains

**Application:**
- Each exercise in the generated workout shows "Last Time" vs "Today's Target"
- Targets are set slightly above last session (e.g., 7.5 kg → 10 kg curls, rod-only → rod + plates)
- If form breaks, hold weight and progress next session — no forced jumps

**Source:** `research/facts-database.md` → Training Programming section; `research/muscle-building/comprehensive-guide.md`

## 3. Full-Body Split, 3x/Week (Day A / Day B Alternation)

**Principle:** Train the entire body across 2 alternating workouts, hitting each muscle group 1.5-2x per week. This maximizes frequency for a beginner without excessive per-session volume.

**Research basis:**
- Muscle protein synthesis (MPS) spikes for 24-48h after training — hitting a muscle every 2-3 days is optimal for beginners (Stronger By Science)
- Full-body splits produce equivalent or superior hypertrophy to bro-splits at the same weekly volume (Jeff Nippard, RP)
- 10-20 hard sets per muscle group per week is the evidence-based range for hypertrophy (RP, Schoenfeld meta-analyses)
- Beginners grow optimally at 10-12 sets/muscle/week

**Application:**
- Day A: Squat focus + pull + press + arms
- Day B: Hinge focus + pull + press + arms (different exercises)
- Alternating ensures every muscle gets stimulated every 3-4 days
- Adapted to gym equipment (Club Viva) from the original home dumbbell/barbell plan

**Source:** `THE-PLAN.md` → Section 2 (Training Program); `research/facts-database.md` → Training Programming

## 4. Exercise Selection: Compound-First, Machine-Adapted

**Principle:** Build workouts around compound (multi-joint) movements, then add isolation work for arms/core. Use whatever equipment is available.

**Research basis:**
- Compound exercises (squats, deadlifts, presses, rows) produce greater overall hypertrophy and strength per unit of training time than isolation-only (Stronger By Science, RP)
- Machine and free-weight exercises produce similar hypertrophy when biomechanically similar — machines allow safer training to failure (Schoenfeld)
- Exercise variety prevents overuse and targets muscles from different angles (Jeff Nippard)

**Application:**
- Workout order: compound (squat, deadlift, chest fly, lat pulldown) → isolation (curls, pushdowns)
- Adapted from home equipment plan to Club Viva machines (lat pulldown, cable row, leg press, chest fly)
- 6-7 exercises per session, covering: quads/hams, chest/back, shoulders, biceps/triceps, core

**Source:** `THE-PLAN.md` → Equipment Audit & Adaptations; `research/muscle-building/comprehensive-guide.md`

## 5. Double Progression Rep Model

**Principle:** Use a rep range (e.g., 8-12 or 10-12). Start at the bottom of the range with a new weight. Add reps each session. When you hit the top of the range on ALL sets, increase weight and drop back reps.

**Research basis:**
- Double progression is the simplest effective progression model for beginners (RP, Eric Helms)
- Training 1-3 reps from failure (RIR 1-3) maximizes hypertrophy while managing fatigue (Schoenfeld, RP)
- Rep ranges of 6-15 produce similar hypertrophy when effort is matched (Schoenfeld meta-analysis)

**Application:**
- Most exercises target 3 × 10
- When all 3 sets hit 10 reps cleanly → increase weight next session
- If you get 6 reps at the new weight before form breaks → that's fine, work up to 10

**Source:** `THE-PLAN.md` → Section 2 (Progression: Double Progression); `research/facts-database.md` → Training Programming

## 6. Volume and Frequency Constraints for Beginner at 130 kg

**Principle:** Keep total per-session volume moderate (6-7 exercises, 3 sets each = ~18-21 sets). This balances stimulus with recovery capacity, especially at higher body weight.

**Research basis:**
- Beginners need less volume to maximize growth — 10-12 sets/muscle/week is sufficient (RP)
- More is not better beyond MEV (Minimum Effective Volume) — junk volume adds fatigue without extra growth (RP)
- At 130 kg with caloric deficit, recovery capacity is reduced — lower volume, higher intensity is superior (RP: "Training During a Cut")
- Reduce training volume ~1/3 during deficit but maintain INTENSITY (proximity to failure) to preserve muscle (RP)

**Application:**
- 3 sets per exercise (not 4-5)
- Rest periods 90s-2.5 min for adequate recovery between sets
- Don't add exercises just to fill time — 6-7 done hard is better than 12 done easy

**Source:** `research/facts-database.md` → Training Programming, Recovery; `research/weight-loss/comprehensive-guide.md`

## 7. Warm-Up Protocol (RAMP Model)

**Principle:** Every session starts with a structured warm-up: Raise → Mobilise → Activate → Potentiate.

**Research basis:**
- Warm-ups improve performance and reduce injury risk (Squat University, Huberman Lab)
- Dynamic warm-ups superior to static stretching pre-workout (meta-analyses)
- Glute activation before squats/deadlifts improves motor recruitment and protects the lower back (Bret Contreras)

**Application:**
- 1-1.5 km walk/run (Raise heart rate, warm tissues)
- Arm circles, torso twists (Mobilise joints)
- Glute bridges (Activate key movers)
- Stretching (toe touch, chest stretch — prepare ROM)
- First working set serves as further warm-up at lighter weight

**Source:** `THE-PLAN.md` → Section 5 (Mobility & Prehab → Warm-Up)

## 8. Specificity to Achyut's Profile

**Principle:** Workouts are tailored to: 33M, 130 kg, 5'11", beginner, with access to Club Viva Gym (₹4K/mo) and prior home equipment.

**Research basis:**
- Training programs must be matched to the individual's experience level, equipment, and physical constraints (Eric Helms, 3DMJ)
- At 130 kg, high-impact activities (running, box jumps) are contraindicated — joints need protection (NIDDK obesity guidelines)
- Beginner adaptations are non-negotiable — advanced techniques (drop sets, rest-pause) are inappropriate until foundation is built (RP)

**Application:**
- No running prescribed (walking instead)
- No jumping/burpees until body weight < 100 kg
- Goblet squats instead of barbell back squats initially (safer for beginner at 130 kg)
- Deadlift starts with rod only, progresses slowly
- All weights start conservative and progress based on demonstrated ability

**Source:** `THE-PLAN.md` → Cardio section; `research/facts-database.md` → Body Recomposition, Psychology

## 9. Workout Logging and Data-Driven Progression

**Principle:** Every set, weight, and rep is logged. Future workouts are generated based on past logged data, not generic templates.

**Research basis:**
- Self-monitoring (food, exercise, weight) is one of the strongest predictors of long-term success (NIDDK, Cochrane)
- Workout logs enable objective progression decisions instead of guesswork (RP)
- Progressive overload can only be applied systematically if you know what happened last time (Stronger By Science)

**Application:**
- `schedule/gym-log.md` tracks all sessions with weights, reps, and notes
- Each generated workout references "Last Time" data to set "Today's Target"
- Progression decisions are data-driven: if you got 10 reps at 10 kg → next time try 12 kg
- If you failed to hit target reps → hold weight, improve reps

**Source:** `THE-PLAN.md` → Section 9 (Progress Tracking); `research/facts-database.md` → Psychology

## 10. Safety and Joint Protection at High Body Weight

**Principle:** At 130 kg, joint health is a limiting factor. No exercise is prescribed that risks injury beyond the benefit it provides.

**Research basis:**
- Ground reaction forces of 7-8x bodyweight during running make it contraindicated at BMI > 35 (NIDDK, ACSM guidelines)
- Goblet squats are safer than barbell squats for beginners — easier to maintain form, self-limiting weight (Squat University)
- Machine-based exercises (chest fly, leg press, lat pulldown) are safer alternatives when learning movement patterns (Schoenfeld)

**Application:**
- Start with machines and lighter free weights
- Goblet squat > barbell back squat (for now)
- Lat pulldown > pull-ups (for now — bands recommended at <100 kg)
- Leg press provides quad stimulus without spinal loading
- If any joint pain → reduce load, check form, add mobility work

**Source:** `THE-PLAN.md` → Cardio section, Equipment Adaptations; `research/facts-database.md`

## 11. Deload and Fatigue Management

**Principle:** Every 4th week, reduce volume ~40-50% to allow recovery. This is preventive, not corrective.

**Research basis:**
- Accumulated fatigue reduces performance and hypertrophy if not managed (RP: Fatigue Management)
- Deload weeks restore performance sensitivity and prevent overuse injuries (Stronger By Science)
- During caloric deficit, fatigue accumulates faster — more frequent deloads may be needed (RP: "Training During a Cut")

**Application:**
- Every 4th week: same exercises, same weight, fewer sets (1-2 instead of 3)
- If persistent fatigue, soreness, or plateau > 2 weeks → early deload
- Track subjective energy levels in gym log notes

**Source:** `THE-PLAN.md` → Section 6 (Sleep & Recovery → Deload Weeks); `research/facts-database.md` → Recovery

## 12. Adaptation to Available Equipment (Home → Gym Transition)

**Principle:** The original plan was designed for home equipment (dumbbells, EZ bar, barbell rod). When Achyut joined Club Viva, exercises were adapted to use superior gym equipment while maintaining the same movement patterns and muscle targets.

**Research basis:**
- Machines and cables provide constant tension through the full ROM, which can be superior to free weights for isolation work (Schoenfeld)
- Lat pulldown machines allow pulling work that's impossible at home without a pull-up bar
- Cable machines provide smooth resistance curves ideal for beginners learning movement patterns (Jeff Nippard)

**Application:**
- Home barbell bent-over row → Lat pulldown + cable row (gym)
- Home floor press → Chest fly machine (gym)
- Home goblet squat → Goblet squat (kept) + Leg press machine (added)
- Home RDL → Deadlift (rod + plates, gym)
- Bicep/tricep work: EZ bar at home → DB curls + cable pushdowns at gym

**Source:** `THE-PLAN.md` → Equipment Audit & Adaptations; `research/facts-database.md` → Training Programming

## Summary: How These Principles Combine

When generating a workout for Achyut, the system:

1. **Reads the gym log** to see what was done last session and what Day (A/B) is next
2. **Checks progression** — can we add weight or reps based on last performance?
3. **Selects exercises** following the compound-first, isolation-second hierarchy
4. **Sets target weights** using double progression logic (small jumps from last session)
5. **Tailors to equipment** available at Club Viva
6. **Respects body weight constraints** — no high-impact, no excessive volume
7. **Includes warm-up and cool-down** per RAMP model
8. **Logs everything** for next session's progression decisions
