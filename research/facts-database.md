## Weight Loss

### Energy Balance & Caloric Deficit
- **Energy balance (calories in vs calories out) is the primary determinant of body weight change.** A calorie surplus is needed for muscle gain and a deficit for fat loss, though body recomposition can occur in beginners. — [Stronger By Science](https://strongerbyscience.com/diet/)
- **A caloric deficit of 20-25% below maintenance is effective for most people.** A deficit of 300-500 kcal/day is a practical starting point. Larger deficits (30%+) increase muscle loss risk. — [Legion Athletics](https://legionathletics.com/how-to-lose-weight-fast/)
- **Fat loss should proceed at 0.5-1.0% of body weight per week.** If below 15% body fat (men) or 25% (women), aim for the lower end (0.5%) to minimize muscle loss. — [Renaissance Periodization](https://rpstrength.com/fat-loss-diet)
- **Low-carb and low-fat diets produce equivalent weight loss at 12 months when calories are matched.** Adherence, not macronutrient composition, is the primary determinant of long-term success. — [Cochrane](https://www.cochranelibrary.com/)

### Protein During Weight Loss
- **Protein intake should be HIGH (2.2-3.1 g/kg) during fat loss phases** to preserve muscle mass. The leaner you get, the higher within this range. — [RP](https://rpstrength.com/fat-loss-diet)
- **High-protein diets preserve metabolic rate better than low-protein diets during weight loss** due to protein's high TEF, muscle-sparing effect, and satiety benefits. — [Legion Athletics](https://legionathletics.com/high-protein-diet/)

### Rate of Loss & Goals
- **A realistic initial weight-loss goal is losing 5% of body weight over 6 months.** For a 200 lb person, this means losing 10 lb. This produces meaningful health improvements. — [NIDDK](https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/weight-management/adult-overweight-obesity/treatment)
- **Evidence-based weight-loss programs include: 14+ counseling sessions over 6 months,** personalized eating plan, ≥150 min/week moderate aerobic activity, daily food/activity monitoring, weekly weight check-ins. — [NIDDK](https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/weight-management/adult-overweight-obesity/treatment)

### Training During Weight Loss
- **During a caloric deficit, reduce training volume (~1/3) but maintain INTENSITY** (proximity to failure, load) to signal the body to keep muscle. — [RP](https://rpstrength.com/training-during-cut)
- **Resistance training during caloric restriction preserves ~2-3 kg of lean mass** compared to diet alone or diet + aerobic exercise. — [Cochrane](https://www.cochranelibrary.com/)
- **Combined diet + exercise interventions produce 2-3 kg more long-term weight loss than diet alone.** — [Cochrane](https://www.cochranelibrary.com/)

### Intermittent Fasting & Meal Timing
- **Intermittent fasting (16:8) produces weight loss equivalent to continuous caloric restriction** when calories are matched. Its primary benefit is simplifying calorie control. — [Legion Athletics](https://legionathletics.com/intermittent-fasting/)
- **Time-restricted eating may preserve slightly more lean mass (~0.5kg) than continuous restriction,** potentially due to higher growth hormone during fasting. — [PubMed 2024 Meta-Analysis](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/)

### Metabolic Adaptation & Reverse Dieting
- **Metabolic adaptation occurs during weight loss:** TDEE decreases by 200-500 kcal/day beyond what weight loss predicts, due to reduced NEAT, RMR, and hormonal changes. — [Weightology](https://weightology.net/metabolic-adaptation/)
- **Reverse dieting (slowly increasing calories by 100-150 kcal/week after a deficit)** can help restore metabolic rate and normalize hormones. — [BioLayne](https://biolaynestore.com/blogs/news)
- **Refeed days (eating at maintenance every 1-2 weeks during extended cuts)** can partially restore metabolic rate, hormone levels, and training performance. — [3D Muscle Journey](https://3dmusclejourney.com/library)

### Self-Monitoring & Maintenance
- **Weight loss maintenance success is strongly associated with consistent self-monitoring** (food tracking, regular weighing). — [Weightology](https://weightology.net/weight-maintenance/)
- **The 'set point' can be lowered through sustained maintenance at a lower weight for 1+ years.** — [Weightology](https://weightology.net/set-point/)

### GLP-1 Medications (2024-2025 Research)
- **GLP-1 receptor agonists (semaglutide, tirzepatide) produce 12-21% body weight reduction over 36-72 weeks.** However, 25-40% of weight lost is lean mass — resistance training and high protein are critical. — [PubMed 2024](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/)
- **Patients on GLP-1 medications who lift weights 2-3x/week preserve ~60% more lean mass** than non-exercisers. — [PubMed 2024](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/)

### Health Risks of Obesity
- **Overweight/obesity increases risk for:** type 2 diabetes, heart disease, stroke, high blood pressure, certain cancers, sleep apnea, osteoarthritis, fatty liver, kidney disease. — [NIDDK](https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/weight-management/adult-overweight-obesity/health-risks)
- **Obesity affects more than 1 in 3 US adults.** Another 1 in 3 is overweight. — [NIDDK](https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/weight-management/adult-overweight-obesity)
- **Globally, 1 in 8 people were living with obesity in 2022** (890 million adults). Adult obesity has more than doubled since 1990. — [WHO](https://www.who.int/news-room/fact-sheets/detail/obesity-and-overweight)

### Activity Recommendations
- **Regular physical activity of at least 150 min/week moderate-intensity is recommended for weight maintenance.** For weight loss, 200-300 min/week may be needed. — [CDC](https://www.cdc.gov/obesity/index.html)
- **7,000-9,000 steps/day are associated with the lowest all-cause mortality risk.** Each additional 1,000 steps/day reduces mortality by ~15% up to ~10,000. — [PubMed 2025](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/)

### Weight-Loss Surgery
- **Bariatric surgery is recommended for BMI ≥35, or BMI ≥30 with serious obesity-related conditions.** It produces 20-30% total body weight loss sustained at 5+ years. — [NIDDK](https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/weight-management/adult-overweight-obesity/treatment)

## Muscle Building

### Volume
- **Training volume is the primary driver of hypertrophy.** MEV: ~6-8 sets/muscle/week. MRV: ~20-25 sets/muscle/week. MAEV (maintenance): ~4-6 sets/week. — [RP](https://rpstrength.com/training-volume-landmarks-muscle-growth)
- **The gluteus maximus is the largest muscle and responds to 15-25 sets/week** with varied angles: hip thrusts, squats, deadlifts, abduction work. — [Bret Contreras](https://bretcontreras.com/glute-training/)

### Mechanical Tension & Stretch
- **Muscle growth is stimulated primarily by mechanical tension.** Exercises that put the muscle under high tension in a stretched position are particularly effective. — [RP](https://rpstrength.com/hypertrophy-training)
- **Training muscles in their stretched position produces ~15-20% more hypertrophy** than full ROM or contracted-position training. Most pronounced for triceps, hamstrings, calves. — [PubMed 2024](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/)
- **Exercise selection should prioritize movements training muscles in lengthened positions** — research shows ~2x more hypertrophy from stretched-position training. — [Jeff Nippard](https://jeffnippard.com/articles/training)

### Rep Range
- **The hypertrophy rep range is 5-30 reps** — any load in this range builds muscle equally well, provided effort (proximity to failure) is matched. — [Jeff Nippard](https://jeffnippard.com/articles/nutrition)
- **A combination of heavy (5-8), moderate (8-15), and light (15-30) rep ranges is optimal** for varied stimulus and injury prevention. — [Jeff Nippard](https://jeffnippard.com/articles/nutrition)

### Frequency
- **Training a muscle 2x/week produces ~30% more hypertrophy than 1x/week** at equal weekly volume. — [Jeff Nippard](https://jeffnippard.com/articles/training)
- **2x/week per muscle group is optimal; 3x/week is not significantly better than 2x** when total weekly volume is equated. — [PubMed 2025 Meta-Analysis](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/)

### Effective Reps & Effort
- **Working sets should be taken to 1-3 RIR (Reps in Reserve).** RIR 2 is the sweet spot. Going to absolute failure on every set increases fatigue without extra growth. — [RP](https://rpstrength.com/effective-reps-hypertrophy-training)
- **The last 5 reps before failure are the most stimulative.** At RIR 3, ~2 reps are "effective"; at RIR 1, ~4 are effective. — [RP](https://rpstrength.com/effective-reps)

### Caloric Surplus for Muscle Gain
- **For hypertrophy, a moderate energy surplus of 200-500 kcal/day facilitates muscle gain** while minimizing fat gain. Excessive surplus leads to unnecessary fat. — [Stronger By Science](https://strongerbyscience.com/diet/)

### Strength & Longevity
- **Strength potential is determined by: muscle size, lift mastery (neurological skill), joint health, and age.** Muscle size is the most modifiable factor. — [SBS](https://strongerbyscience.com/complete-strength-training-guide/)
- **Resistance training preserves muscle mass (sarcopenia prevention), bone density, and metabolic health.** Muscle mass is inversely associated with all-cause mortality. — [Peter Attia](https://peterattiamd.com/resistance-training-longevity/)
- **Grip strength is a powerful biomarker: each 5kg decrease is associated with 16% increase in all-cause mortality risk.** — [Peter Attia](https://peterattiamd.com/grip-strength/)
- **Hypertrophy response has a genetic component (40-70% heritability), but consistent training produces meaningful results in >95% of individuals.** — [PubMed 2025](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/)

### Range of Motion
- **Full ROM is generally superior to partial ROM for hypertrophy.** However, lengthened partials (bottom half of movement) can be equally or more effective. — [Jeff Nippard](https://jeffnippard.com/articles/training)

### Mind-Muscle Connection
- **Internal focus on the target muscle during exercise increases activation by 10-20%.** Most beneficial for isolation exercises and stubborn body parts. — [T-Nation](https://t-nation.com/training/mind-muscle-connection/)

## Nutrition

### Protein
- **Optimal protein intake: 1.6-2.2 g/kg body weight/day for resistance-trained individuals.** Above 2.2 g/kg shows diminishing returns. — [Examine.com](https://examine.com/guides/protein/)
- **Per-meal protein: 0.4-0.55 g/kg (30-50g for 75-90kg person) across 4 meals maximizes muscle protein synthesis.** — [PubMed 2024](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/)
- **Each meal should contain ~2-3g leucine to trigger the MPS threshold.** Below this, muscle protein synthesis is not maximally stimulated. — [Examine.com](https://examine.com/supplements/leucine/)
- **Animal proteins (dairy, egg, meat, fish) are higher quality than plant proteins** — more leucine, EAAs, better digestibility. — [SBS](https://strongerbyscience.com/diet/)
- **Vegan diets can match omnivorous hypertrophy if:** 3-4 protein meals/day, ≥0.24g/kg per meal, ≥2-3g leucine per meal, complementary amino acids. — [SBS](https://strongerbyscience.com/diet/)

### Protein Timing
- **Protein timing (within 1 hour post-workout) provides no significant hypertrophy advantage** over consuming the same protein at other times, when total daily protein is adequate. — [PubMed 2024](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/)
- **Distribute protein across 3-4 meals spaced 3-5 hours apart** due to the 'muscle full' effect where MPS tapers after feeding. — [SBS](https://strongerbyscience.com/diet/)
- **30-40g casein before sleep increases overnight muscle protein synthesis by ~22%.** — [Examine.com](https://examine.com/supplements/casein/)

### Nutrition Priority Pyramid
- **Priority order: 1) Energy balance, 2) Macronutrients, 3) Micronutrients, 4) Nutrient timing, 5) Supplements.** Focus on the base before the apex. — [3D Muscle Journey](https://3dmusclejourney.com/library)

### Macronutrient Energy Values
- **Protein: 4 kcal/g. Carbohydrate: 4 kcal/g. Fat: 9 kcal/g. Alcohol: 7 kcal/g.** — [SBS](https://strongerbyscience.com/diet/)

### Fiber
- **Fiber intake of 25-35g/day from diverse sources** supports gut microbiome, improves satiety, associated with lower body fat independently of calorie intake. — [BioLayne](https://biolaynestore.com/blogs/news)

### Pre/Post-Workout Nutrition
- **Pre-workout: 30-60g easily digestible carbs + 20-40g protein, 60-90 min before training** to top off glycogen and amino acids. — [Vertical Diet](https://www.verticaldiet.com/articles)
- **Sodium intake of 3000-5000 mg/day is appropriate for athletes** who train hard and sweat. Restricting sodium impairs performance and pump. — [Vertical Diet](https://www.verticaldiet.com/articles)

### Caffeine Timing
- **Timing caffeine 90-120 minutes after waking prevents afternoon crash** and optimizes ergogenic effect for mid-morning workouts. — [Huberman Lab](https://hubermanlab.com/caffeine/)

### Hydration
- **Dehydration of just 2% body weight reduces exercise performance by 10-20%.** For a 75kg person, this is only 1.5L of fluid. — [Stronger By Science](https://strongerbyscience.com/diet/)

### Hard Gainer Strategy
- **Liquid calories (whole milk, shakes with oats/whey/PB) are efficient for hard gainers.** 500-1000 kcal of liquid is much easier to consume than equivalent solid food. — [Vertical Diet](https://www.verticaldiet.com/articles)

## Supplements

### Creatine
- **Creatine monohydrate is the most researched and effective supplement for strength/muscle.** Dose: 3-5g daily. Loading phase (20g/day for 5-7 days) optional. — [Examine.com](https://examine.com/supplements/creatine/)
- **Creatine increases muscle phosphocreatine by 10-40%, enhancing ATP availability.** Translates to ~5-15% improvements in maximal power output. — [Examine.com](https://examine.com/supplements/creatine/)
- **Creatine is most effective taken consistently every day (3-5g), regardless of workout timing.** Small benefit to taking with carbs (insulin enhances uptake). — [Examine.com](https://examine.com/supplements/creatine/)

### Caffeine
- **Caffeine enhances exercise performance at 3-6 mg/kg, 30-60 min pre-exercise.** Improves endurance, power output, and focus. Habituation reduces effect. — [Examine.com](https://examine.com/supplements/caffeine/)

### Whey Protein
- **Whey protein is the highest quality supplemental protein** (BV 104, high leucine ~10-11%). Rapidly absorbed, ideal post-workout. — [Examine.com](https://examine.com/supplements/whey-protein/)
- **Whey isolate (90%+ protein) vs concentrate (70-80%)**: isolate preferred for lactose sensitivity or strict calorie cutting. — [Examine.com](https://examine.com/supplements/whey-protein/)

### Other Supplements
- **Beta-alanine (3.2-6.4g/day) improves performance for 60-240 second activities** by buffering lactic acid. Benefits are ~1-3%. — [Examine.com](https://examine.com/supplements/beta-alanine/)
- **Fish oil (2-4g EPA+DHA daily) may reduce muscle soreness and inflammation.** Direct effect on muscle growth is minimal. — [Examine.com](https://examine.com/supplements/fish-oil/)
- **Vitamin D: ~40-60% of populations are deficient.** Associated with reduced muscle function, lower testosterone, impaired recovery. Supplement 1000-4000 IU daily. — [Examine.com](https://examine.com/supplements/vitamin-d/)
- **Citrulline malate (6-8g pre-workout) reduces fatigue and increases training volume** via nitric oxide production and ammonia clearance. — [Examine.com](https://examine.com/supplements/citrulline/)

## Recovery

### Sleep
- **Optimal sleep for fitness recovery: 7-9 hours/night.** Sleep deprivation (<6h) reduces testosterone 10-15%, impairs recovery, increases cortisol, reduces insulin sensitivity, increases hunger. — [Huberman Lab](https://hubermanlab.com/toolkits-for-sleep/)
- **Sleep deprivation (<6 hours) reduces strength performance by 5-10% and delays recovery by 30-50%.** — [PubMed 2025 Meta-Analysis](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/)

### Deload
- **Deload weeks (reduce volume ~40-60% for one week) every 4-8 weeks** dissipate fatigue and allow supercompensation. Preventive, not corrective. — [Legion Athletics](https://legionathletics.com/deload/)

### Circadian Rhythm & Light
- **Viewing bright light within 30-60 min of waking triggers cortisol/dopamine release**, enhancing alertness and circadian rhythm, indirectly supporting training and recovery. — [Huberman Lab](https://hubermanlab.com/toolkits-for-sleep/)

### Cold Exposure
- **Cold exposure (50-60°F for 1-3 min) increases dopamine 250% and norepinephrine 530%.** However, chronic cold exposure immediately after resistance training may blunt hypertrophy. — [Huberman Lab](https://hubermanlab.com/cold-exposure/)

## Metabolism

### TDEE Components
- **TDEE composition: BMR (~70%), TEF (~10%), EAT (~5%), NEAT (~15%).** — [SBS](https://strongerbyscience.com/diet/)
- **NEAT varies by up to 2000 kcal/day between individuals** and is the most variable TDEE component. During dieting, body subconsciously reduces NEAT. — [Weightology](https://weightology.net/neat/)

### TEF (Thermic Effect of Feeding)
- **TEF: Protein ~20-30%, Carbs ~5-10%, Fat ~0-3%** of calories consumed. — [SBS](https://strongerbyscience.com/diet/)

### EPOC (Afterburn)
- **EPOC from high-intensity exercise is small (~6-15% of exercise calories)** — not enough to compensate for poor diet. — [Legion Athletics](https://legionathletics.com/afterburn-effect/)

### Metabolic Adaptation Reality
- **The biggest factor in weight loss plateaus is decreased body mass** (less tissue = lower TDEE), not a 'damaged metabolism.' Solution: adjust calories downward or increase activity. — [BioLayne](https://biolaynestore.com/blogs/news)

## Training Programming

### Beginner Training
- **Beginner priorities: buy-in/habit formation, movement proficiency, body awareness, recovery readiness.** Beginners gain muscle doing almost anything. — [SBS](https://strongerbyscience.com/complete-strength-training-guide/)

### Periodization
- **Progressive overload should be mesocycle-to-mesocycle.** Within a 3-6 week mesocycle, volume accumulates from MEV toward MRV, then a deload drops volume ~50%. — [RP](https://rpstrength.com/periodization-muscle-growth)
- **Specialization blocks (focusing 1-2 muscle groups at high volume, others at MEV) produce better results than trying to improve everything at once.** 4-8 week blocks typical. — [RP](https://rpstrength.com/specialization-blocks)

### Rest Intervals
- **Rest 2-3 minutes between sets for optimal hypertrophy and strength.** Shorter rest (30-60s) reduces performable volume without extra benefit. — [Jeff Nippard](https://jeffnippard.com/articles/training)

### Progressive Overload Methods
- **Progressive overload = increasing weight, reps, sets, improving form/ROM, or decreasing rest.** Weight on the bar is just one tool. — [Legion Athletics](https://legionathletics.com/progressive-overload/)

### Stimulus-to-Fatigue Ratio
- **Exercises with poor SFR (barbell skull crushers, heavy behind-the-neck press) should be replaced** with better alternatives as a mesocycle progresses. — [RP](https://rpstrength.com/stimulus-to-fatigue-ratio)
- **Fatigue: local (muscle-specific, desired) vs systemic (CNS/overall, must be managed).** High-rep squats create excessive systemic fatigue relative to hypertrophy benefit. — [RP](https://rpstrength.com/fatigue-management)

### Intensity Techniques
- **Drop sets, rest-pause, supersets increase metabolic stress but should be used sparingly** (1-2x per mesocycle) to avoid overtraining. — [T-Nation](https://t-nation.com/training/intensity-techniques/)
- **Training to failure is more appropriate on isolation exercises (curls, extensions, flyes)** than compounds (squats, deadlifts, bench). — [T-Nation](https://t-nation.com/training/failure-guide/)

### Cardio for Health
- **Zone 2 cardio (60-70% max HR, conversational pace): 180+ min/week recommended** for mitochondrial function and metabolic health. — [Peter Attia](https://peterattiamd.com/exercise-longevity/)
- **VO2 max: moving from bottom 25% to top 25% of cardiorespiratory fitness reduces all-cause mortality risk by ~50-70%.** — [Peter Attia](https://peterattiamd.com/vo2-max/)
- **Huberman's 'Fundamental Five':** 1) 150-200 min/week Zone 2, 2) 75+ min/week VO2 max, 3) 2-3x/week resistance training, 4) 150-200 jumps/week for bone density, 5) 10-15 min/day mobility. — [Huberman Lab](https://hubermanlab.com/fitness-protocols/)

### WHO Physical Activity Guidelines
- **Adults: 150-300 min/week moderate OR 75-150 min/week vigorous aerobic activity,** plus muscle-strengthening 2+ days/week. — [WHO](https://www.who.int/news-room/fact-sheets/detail/obesity-and-overweight)

### Auto-Regulation
- **Auto-regulation (RPE/RIR) should modulate within a planned range, not replace structured programming.** — [3D Muscle Journey](https://3dmusclejourney.com/library)

### Variable Resistance
- **Banded/cable exercises match muscle strength curves better than free weights alone,** useful for glutes and delts. — [Bret Contreras](https://bretcontreras.com/resistance-bands/)

## Body Recomposition

### Feasibility
- **Body recomposition is most feasible in: untrained individuals, high body fat individuals, those returning from training layoffs.** Trained/lean individuals have minimal recomp potential. — [SBS](https://strongerbyscience.com/diet/)
- **Trained individuals CAN recomp, but magnitude is small:** ~0.5-1% body fat reduction with ~0.5-1kg lean mass gain over 8-16 weeks. — [PubMed 2024](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/)

## Psychology

### Adherence & Sustainability
- **Flexible dieting (tracking macros, not restricting foods) produces better long-term adherence than rigid dieting.** Psychological sustainability = physiological results. — [BioLayne](https://biolaynestore.com/blogs/news)
- **Habit formation and community support are the most important factors for new lifters' long-term success** — more important than program optimization. — [SBS](https://strongerbyscience.com/complete-strength-training-guide/)

## BMI Classification

- **WHO: Overweight = BMI ≥25, Obesity = BMI ≥30.** For Asian populations: overweight ≥23, obesity ≥27.5 (lower thresholds due to higher metabolic risk). — [WHO](https://www.who.int/news-room/fact-sheets/detail/obesity-and-overweight)
- **Obesity is classified by WHO as a chronic, relapsing disease** involving genetics, neurobiology, eating behaviors, environment, and market forces. — [WHO](https://www.who.int/news-room/fact-sheets/detail/obesity-and-overweight)

### Additional Weight Loss Facts
- **Very low calorie diets (<800 kcal/day) produce faster initial weight loss but result in greater muscle loss** and have higher dropout rates. At 12 months, outcomes are similar to moderate dieting. — [Cochrane](https://www.cochranelibrary.com/)
- **Meal replacements (shakes/bars replacing 1-2 meals/day) produce greater short-term weight loss (3-5 kg more at 3 months)** than conventional reduced-calorie diets, due to improved portion control. — [Cochrane](https://www.cochranelibrary.com/)
- **Stress management is key for weight management:** chronic stress elevates cortisol, which promotes visceral fat storage, increases cravings, and reduces exercise motivation. — [CDC](https://www.cdc.gov/obesity/risk-factors/risk-factors.html)
- **Obesity disproportionately affects certain demographics:** non-Hispanic Black adults (49.6%) and Hispanic adults (45.6%) have higher obesity prevalence than non-Hispanic White adults (39.8%) in the US. — [CDC](https://www.cdc.gov/obesity/data-and-statistics/adult-obesity-prevalence-maps.html)
- **The NIDDK Body Weight Planner shows weight loss is non-linear:** a 500 kcal/day deficit does NOT produce 1 lb/week loss indefinitely — expect ~50% less than predicted by 6 months due to metabolic adaptation. — [NIDDK](https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/weight-management/body-weight-planner)
- **Waist circumference is an independent risk factor for metabolic disease:** >94 cm (37") for men and >80 cm (31.5") for women indicates increased risk. — [WHO](https://www.who.int/news-room/fact-sheets/detail/obesity-and-overweight)
- **Childhood obesity tracks strongly into adulthood:** children with obesity are 5x more likely to have obesity as adults. — [WHO](https://www.who.int/news-room/fact-sheets/detail/obesity-and-overweight)

### Additional Muscle Building Facts
- **Eccentric-only training (lowering phase) produces similar hypertrophy to concentric-eccentric** at matched volume, but with less fatigue. Concentric-only produces less hypertrophy. — [PubMed 2024](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/)
- **Blood flow restriction (BFR) training using light loads (20-30% 1RM) with cuffs can produce hypertrophy comparable to heavy training.** Useful during injury rehab or deloads. — [Jeff Nippard](https://jeffnippard.com/articles/training)
- **Stretch-mediated hypertrophy: holding a muscle under stretch for 30-60 seconds per set** can increase hypertrophy beyond what dynamic training alone achieves. — [Jeff Nippard](https://jeffnippard.com/articles/training)
- **For advanced lifters (3+ years consistent training), hypertrophy becomes extremely slow (~0.5-1 kg lean mass per year).** Training sophistication becomes critical. — [RP](https://rpstrength.com/advanced-training)
- **For bone health, weight-bearing exercise at >85% 1RM stimulates osteogenesis.** Low-load exercise does not meaningfully improve bone density. — [Peter Attia](https://peterattiamd.com/resistance-training-longevity/)

### Additional Nutrition Facts
- **Pre-sleep protein (30-40g casein) combined with resistance training increases overnight MPS by ~20-30%** and can enhance long-term hypertrophy by ~5-10%. — [PubMed 2025](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/)
- **Intra-workout nutrition (5-15g EAAs or 20-40g carbs) during sessions lasting 75+ minutes** can delay fatigue and increase total volume by 5-10%. — [RP](https://rpstrength.com/intra-workout-nutrition)
- **Post-workout: 30-50g protein + 30-80g carbs within 2 hours can modestly enhance glycogen replenishment and MPS.** — [Vertical Diet](https://www.verticaldiet.com/articles)
- **Alcohol provides 7 kcal/g and its metabolism takes priority over fat oxidation**, significantly blunting fat loss even within caloric targets. — [SBS](https://strongerbyscience.com/diet/)

### Additional Recovery Facts
- **Sleep duration is inversely associated with BMI:** each hour below 7 hours/night is associated with 0.35 kg/m² increase in BMI. — [CDC](https://www.cdc.gov/obesity/risk-factors/risk-factors.html)
- **CNS recovery takes 48-72 hours after heavy compound training** (vs 24-48h for muscles), explaining why heavy full-body sessions 3+ days in a row cause staleness. — [T-Nation](https://t-nation.com/training/recovery/)
- **Tart cherry juice (8-12 oz, 2x/day) reduces DOMS, oxidative stress, and inflammation.** Works via anthocyanins. — [Vertical Diet](https://www.verticaldiet.com/articles)

### Additional Supplement Facts
- **Collagen peptides (10-15g daily) may improve joint health and tendon strength** over 3-6 months. Not effective for muscle building (low leucine). — [Examine.com](https://examine.com/supplements/collagen/)
- **Ashwagandha (300-600mg KSM-66 daily) reduces cortisol ~15-25% and may modestly increase testosterone** (10-15%) in stressed/trained men. — [Examine.com](https://examine.com/supplements/ashwagandha/)
- **Zinc deficiency (~17% globally) can reduce testosterone.** Supplementing 15-30mg daily may benefit intense-training athletes. — [Examine.com](https://examine.com/supplements/zinc/)
- **Magnesium (200-400mg glycinate/citrate) can improve sleep and recovery** in deficient individuals (~50% of adults). — [Examine.com](https://examine.com/supplements/magnesium/)

### Additional Psychology Facts
- **Diet breaks (1-2 weeks at maintenance during extended cuts) do not impair total fat loss and significantly improve psychological well-being, hunger management, and program adherence.** — [PubMed 2024](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/)
